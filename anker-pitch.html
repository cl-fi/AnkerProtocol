Why APR Can Be Structurally Better

Four reasons more option premium can reach the user.

1. Incentives reversed
CEX earns more when it pays less.
Anker earns 10% of coupon, so higher user coupon also raises Anker fee.
2. One explicit fee
10% of coupon only.
No principal fee. Net APR is shown after fee; market costs are visible.
3. Market replication
DeepBook legs recreate the payoff.
No CEX risk desk warehousing risk and adding markup to the quote.
4. Transparency caps margin
Benchmarks and leg costs are visible.
Visible pricing compresses hidden middleman margin.
3:25-3:56. Now this fee model explains why our APR can be structurally better than a CEX quote. First, incentives are reversed. A CEX is the counterparty, so it earns more when it pays users less. Anker only earns 10 percent of the coupon, so a higher coupon helps both the user and Anker. Second, the fee is simple and explicit: 10 percent of coupon only, not principal, and the APR is already net. Third, Anker recreates the payoff from live DeepBook Predict legs instead of running a risk desk that warehouses risk and adds markup. Fourth, transparency caps margin: the Binance benchmark and leg costs are visible, so hidden margin gets compressed.
